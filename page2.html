Taylor Swift's Album Discography

Evermore

Evermore is Swift’s 9th studio album and was the second surprise release of 2020. Released in December, multiple songs remark on being heartbroken around the holidays with lyrics like “they’ll deck the halls that we once walked through” and “the holidays linger like bad perfume, you can run, but only so far.” Once again, Swift wrote many of these songs from the perspective of others, drawing inspiration from books and movies. Other tracks on evermore include stories of young con artists falling in love, Dorothea, a girl who left her hometown friends to chase big dreams, a trilogy of marriages gone bad, and even memories of Swift’s late grandmother. Finally, evermore is nominated for the 2022 Album of the Year award at the Grammys.

Folklore

After cutting the Lover era short due to Covid-19, Swift took everyone by storm and released a surprise album in July of 2020 in one of her unexplored genres: Indie/Folk. Written, recorded, and produced in quarantine, the Folklore stories are mostly fictional- which was new territory for Taylor because she typically writes about her own experiences. Lyrically, what makes Folklore most unique is a love triangle between three imaginary teenagers. Each person has their own song- “Betty,” “August,” and “Cardigan”- in which the thoughts, fears, and regrets of each are expressed through poetic lyrics and cathartic melodies. Folklore won Taylor her third Album of the Year award at the Grammys.

Lover

The Lover era was an explosion of pastel colors, sparkly hearts, and themes of love and all it entails. Lover explores all sides and stages of love: infatuation, commitment, anxiety, lust, equality, longing, reconciliation, safety, etc. The order of tracks on Lover is also very intentional, beginning with “I Forgot That You Existed,” a song about how you can wake up one day and realize that someone or something that used to have power over you no longer does. The final track, “Daylight,” symbolizes looking away from the darkness that was reputation and looking toward the light. She chose it as the last track because “it recognizes past damage and pain but shows that it doesn’t have to define you.”

Reputation

Reputation was Swift’s comeback album. Throughout 2016, Taylor was involved in multiple controversies ultimately leading her to go off the grid for a year. With lead single “Look What You Made Me Do,” Swift came back strong by embracing snake imagery, dark color palettes, and the concept of a bad “reputation.” Songs on reputation are her most edgy, as she explored electronic and techno music genres. Critics were not as impressed with this as her previous work, but the reputation “era” was necessary for Swift to get back into dominating the mainstream music scene once again.

1989

1989 has been described by critics as the “perfect pop album.” By this album, which is named after the year Swift was born, she was a full blown pop-star. Whether people loved or hated her, they all had an opinion: she was too skinny, she dated too many guys, she was a crazy ex-girlfriend, her friend group was too exclusive, etc. Nevertheless, these criticisms inspired arguably the two biggest hits of her career: “Shake It Off” and “Blank Space.” 1989 also won Swift her second Album of the Year at the Grammys.

Red (Taylor's Version)

Swift has said that “lyrically and sonically, Red resembles a heart-broken person.” As her hit track 22 puts it, she and her friends were “happy, free, confused and lonely at the same time.” As a result, there is not a specific genre of music on Red, but rather, there is everything from pop melodies to acoustic rhythms. Swift also did her first collaboration on Red, which was with Ed Sheeran in the track “Everything Has Changed.” More artists were featured on the recent re-release of Red, including Chris Stapleton on “I Bet You Think About Me” and Phoebe Bridgers on “Nothing New.”

Speak Now

After being the subject of some skepticism about her songwriting, Swift decided to be the only writer on her 3rd album. That’s right- there were no co-writers on Speak Now. Still in the country genre, Speak Now is a little more relaxed than Fearless, though there are still very bubbly tracks like “Mine,” “Speak Now,” and “Sparks Fly.” However, this album is when fans really got to hear the first of many heart-wrenching ballads like “Back To December,” “Dear John,” and “Last Kiss.” Swift finished the Speak Now era with a global tour that lasted for more than a year.

Fearless (Taylor's Version)

Swift’s sophomore album is what really put her on the map. She stuck to her country roots but began to explore the pop verse with some of her biggest hits to date: “Love Story” and “You Belong With Me”. Sonically and lyrically, Fearless pushes naive and hopelessly romantic motifs. Nevertheless, these daydreams might have been the reason they resonated with so many young people around the globe. Additionally, Fearless was the first album Swift re-recorded on her quest to reclaim her music for herself after it was bought out from under her. More on that in Recent News. Finally, Fearless won Swift her first Album of the Year at the Grammys.

Debut

Taylor Swift’s self-titled debut album came out in October 2006 when she was just 16 years old. Her first single, “Tim McGraw,” gave indication that she would start her music career in the Country genre- a natural beginning for her considering she moved to Nashville when she was 12 to pursue country music. Though her journey as a songwriter was just beginning, she showed incredible potential in these lyrics by being the sole writer on hit tracks like “Our Song.” Swift never toured for this album, but she still plays these beloved tunes in concerts today.
